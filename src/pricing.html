How Much Does A Website Cost?

The cost of a website depends on a variety of factors. While the cost of a website, in general, can range from $1,000 to $100,000 - the cost of your website depends on what your needs are.

Major Factors That Influence Pricing

Size

$120-1000

The size of a website is generally defined by the number of pages or sections. In most cases, the price per page scales off as the number of pages grows.

Per Page, Essential
Style

$200-1000

Style defines the visual aesthetic of your website. Simple (yet attractive) styles by us cost less while our high-end styling service costs more.

Essential Add-on
Copywriting

$20-500

Copywriting refers to writing content that captures your brand, product features and unique selling points. Optimized copywriting helps create an effective website.

Per Page, Add-on
SEO

$100-1000

SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. Search engines can be a critical resource that help bring more people to your website.

Add-on
Custom Features

$50-25000

Have a custom feature request like an image slider, a specific contact form? The cost of each feature depends on the complexity and time required to build it.

Add-on
CMS Integration

$300-2000

CMS stands for Content Management System. It allows you to easily update or add content like blogs to your website. Managing a CMS requires skill.

Add-on
Tracking

$50-800

Implementing tracking allows you to see the performance of your website in terms of number of visitors, popularity in a region and more.

Add-on
Domain & Hosting

$24-20000

Your domain name acts as the address to your website while hosting is what serves your website on the Internet. The charges for these are periodical.
